A Rich Heritage

History of Winchester

Winchester's history spans millennia, from an Iron Age hillfort to a major Roman city and the capital of Anglo-Saxon Wessex under King Alfred the Great. It was a crucial medieval center, home to magnificent cathedrals and palaces, a royal wedding and later, the final resting place for Jane Austen. The city blends its ancient Roman grid, Saxon foundations, Norman structures, and Georgian elegance, remaining a significant historical English city.
